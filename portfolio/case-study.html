Professional case study

Building a bilingual, local-first operations agent for low-resource health facilities.

A practical demonstration of deterministic tools, optional generative AI, clinical safety, evaluation, interoperability, and deployment readiness.

Executive summary

AfyaIntel helps authorized facility staff monitor medicine and supply stock, identify expiry risks, and draft English or Swahili operations reports. It is designed to keep essential workflows available when connectivity or model quota is unavailable.

The problem

Small facilities may rely on paper registers, disconnected spreadsheets, and manual reporting. Staff can spend time identifying shortages, checking expiry dates, and preparing supervisor updates. A cloud-only assistant introduces additional dependence on cost, connectivity, and quota.

The solution

  • Validated inventory ingestion
  • Low-stock and expiry analysis
  • English and Swahili summaries
  • Weekly management reports
  • Clinical-safety and emergency routing
  • Local fallback and privacy-safe audit metadata

Architecture

The orchestrator applies language routing and a deterministic clinical-safety check before selecting a stock, expiry, report, or item-lookup tool. Gemini is called only when no local operational tool matches and the request is non-clinical. A quota or network failure returns a local fallback.

Evaluation

The project includes 14 zero-API evaluation checks and 10 Python unit tests. The tests verify inventory accuracy, bilingual routing, safety refusal, emergency escalation, invalid-data rejection, audit privacy, and tool-contract integrity.

Responsible scope

AfyaIntel uses synthetic data, does not diagnose or prescribe, and cannot autonomously change inventory, order medicines, pay suppliers, or communicate externally. Human approval remains mandatory.

Outcome

The completed prototype demonstrates a credible path from course learning to a governed operational pilot while remaining honest about pending external codelab evidence and production approvals.
