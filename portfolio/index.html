The challenge

Stock decisions are difficult when records, connectivity, and reporting are fragmented.

Small facilities may depend on paper registers or disconnected spreadsheets. Staff must manually identify shortages, review expiry dates, and prepare updates for supervisors.

A cloud-only assistant also introduces a second risk: the product can stop working when the network or free-tier model quota is unavailable.

The solution

A deterministic operational core with an optional generative layer.

01

Validated inventory

Rejects missing columns, invalid dates, negative quantities, and mixed-facility records.

02

Shortage and expiry tools

Computes threshold breaches and 30-day expiry risk from structured data—not model memory.

03

English and Swahili

Produces concise summaries and weekly reports for bilingual operational workflows.

04

Clinical safety gateway

Blocks diagnosis, prescription, dosage, treatment, and emergency requests before any model call.

05

Quota resilience

Core tools remain available locally when Gemini, connectivity, or quota is unavailable.

06

Human accountability

Procurement, redistribution, communication, payment, and clinical decisions remain with authorized people.
